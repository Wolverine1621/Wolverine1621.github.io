Death Amongst Modern Migrators

Migrators take a small boat from Cuba to the Florida Keys

Today, hope is one of the most valuable resources to humanity. Especially to those whose home is ruled by a vicious leader, under harsh circumstances, or the victim of a calamity or natural disaster. Those under these circumstances have an extremely low level of choices. To get by in the conditions, to attempt to fix the conditions, or the most common: to leave the conditions. Those who leave the conditions, are the few modern migrators left over today. Although, sadly, the measurement of “few” migrators, has turned to a complete global brouhaha of migration to and from new homes -- and the road between homes is not a peaceful or simple one. Thousands of migrators die every year. Far from the leading causes of death, but also an extremely harsh cause of death, migration can cause dehydration, starvation, malnutrition, disease, and even being killed by the coastal guards of the new homes of the migrators.

A discarded shoe at the Mexico/New Mexico Border

- Over 6,000 migrators die daily attempting to move to a new place.

- Only an approximate 45% of migrators make it to their destination.

- The amount of migrators dying per year has increased at about 500 people a year.
